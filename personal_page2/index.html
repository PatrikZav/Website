About

Patrik Zavadil is a high school student from Kroměříž, Czech Republic, passionate about programming, web development, and fitness. He’s on a mission to build a life of freedom for himself and his family through technology and discipline. With a clear vision and strong work ethic, he’s committed to turning ambition into reality. Patrik represents a new generation determined to succeed on their own terms.

Early Life

Patrik Zavadil was born on November 30, 2007, in Kroměříž, Czech Republic, where he was raised in a calm and supportive environment. Like many kids, he grew up fascinated by heroes — both fictional and real. In 2024, a curiosity sparked by gaming and online videos led him into the world of programming and web development.


Patrik began creating digital projects and videos, many of which are now featured on his personal website. While still in high school, he balances schoolwork with regular gym sessions five times a week, building discipline both mentally and physically. Inspired by self-made individuals like Iman Gadzhi, Jan Šrajer, David Luu, and Jan Liška, Patrik is driven by a desire for independence and a commitment to provide a better future for his family. With a clear focus and strong ambition, he’s steadily building the foundations of a life on his own terms.

Projects

YouTube

Founded on January 1, 2014, Patrik Zavadil’s YouTube channel Pazaxcz features montages from video games, car edits, and glimpses of his life, including go-kart racing. The channel combines high-energy content with personal insights, aiming to entertain and inspire viewers.

First personal web page

Patrik's first website was created after just three months of studying IT, marking a significant milestone in his journey as a web developer. Although some sections were left incomplete, it served as an important learning experience that laid the foundation for his future projects.

BMW M4 website

Patrik's BMW M4 website was created as part of a school project after seven months of studying IT. This project stands as his most sophisticated work to date, showcasing his growing skills in web development without the use of Java.
